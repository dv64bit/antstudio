what's your coolant?

Traditionally natural cooling techniques have been used for thousands of years before mechanical cooling systems were invented in the previous century. As a startup, our vision is to re-adapt traditional methods of cooling using new tools and technological research, in support of initiatives against global warming. CoolAnt products consume less energy and no chemicals. Unlike other conventional cooling systems such as air conditioners and air coolers, that consume high amounts of energy and water, Coolant is an innovative practice that is both energy and water-efficient.

the cooling paradox

Cooling the World is warming the Planet.

Apart from harmful emissions and elevating global warming potential, air conditioning is only limited to closed and indoor spaces. The inherent functioning of air conditioners releases heat into the surroundings, thus creating ‘heat islands’ in cities. Increase in temperatures in turn demands more cooling in our built spaces. Emissions and heat dissipations have a ‘butterfly effect’ in warming up the macro-climate, leading to what we can call the ‘Cooling Paradox’.

Perspiration & Health

With modern lifestyles our bodies are becoming inactive and fragile. This affects natural perspiration and metabolism of individuals in the long run. Natural cooling and passive design solutions help regulate body temperature as per varying weather conditions. These systems give us adequate thermal comfort to keep us more healthy and resistant to heat.

Go Local for Emission Free Spaces

It is scientifically proven that the air quality in our closed indoors spaces is worse than outdoor spaces. The paint that we use, furniture and cooking emit gases in our building. Shifting to local materials is economical and reduces impact on our environment too! Natural ventilation keeps the air fresh and is Good for Health, Good for Environment.

Insulation & Double Skin

Double skins have been a part of many traditional buildings in hot and dry climate. Like an onion, multiple layers keep the inner layer more protected. The porous outer layer of the building (jali), like onion skin adds insulation and allows complete movement of air blocking the sunlight. With modern techniques this traditional element can be used in many better ways.

%

Rainwater Harvesting

Earth is 70% water. We are not short of water, but only 1% of it is accessible for drinking.  With water becoming scarce in cities, if we carefully collect the water and store it, we can use it for drinking as well as other day activities like cooking, cleaning and also cooling! Rainwater harvesting systems are necessary in buildings to maintain the local groundwater.
